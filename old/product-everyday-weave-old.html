Production Story

The Weavers

Each Everyday Weave is handcrafted by women weavers in Buhi, Camarines Sur, using traditional handloom techniques passed down through generations. The weaving process takes approximately 6-8 hours per piece, depending on the complexity of the pattern.

Upcycled Materials

We source upcycled cotton threads from textile factories, diverting waste from landfills and reducing environmental impact. These threads are sorted, cleaned, and prepared by women in our network, creating additional income opportunities.

Traditional Patterns

The striped design reflects traditional Bicolano weaving aesthetics, with each weaver adding her own interpretation to the color placement and pattern rhythm. This maintains cultural heritage while allowing for individual creativity.

Climate Resilience

Handloom weaving can continue even during power outages and typhoon recovery periods, providing stable income when other work is disrupted. This flexibility is critical for climate-vulnerable communities in Bicol.
